Wednesday 19 November 2025

Rory McPhail, promising young political figure and cadet leader, dies aged 18

The Holy Spirit College Year 12 student from Mackay had already built an impressive resume in conservative politics and youth leadership before his death by suicide

By Matthew Denholm, Queensland Correspondent
Wednesday 19 November 2025 12:30 AEDT
Rory McPhail, 18, had been widely regarded as one of Mackay’s most promising young conservatives.

Rory McPhail, an ambitious Year 12 student at Holy Spirit College in Mount Pleasant, Mackay, who had already met several senior federal and state politicians and held leadership roles in youth politics and the Australian Army Cadets, has died by suicide. He was 18.

Widely seen as a future political candidate, McPhail had worked as a staffer for federal Dawson MP Andrew Willcox and Queensland state member for Whitsunday Amanda Camm. He was a member of the 2024 Mackay Youth Council and had recently been promoted to Cadet Under Officer in the 122 Army Cadet Unit (Mackay Region).

From an early age he showed a keen interest in public life. In 2019, while still in Grade 6, he met One Nation leader Pauline Hanson. In the years that followed he sought out meetings with Opposition Leader Peter Dutton and Queensland Premier David Crisafulli.

Friends and colleagues described him as driven, articulate and deeply committed to community service and conservative values.

He is survived by his parents, Emma and Matt McPhail, and his younger sister, Rubi.

A private funeral will be held in Mackay in the coming days.

Support is available from Lifeline on 13 11 14.
